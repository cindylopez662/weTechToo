Check out your bias!


    "Women need to behave like men when working in STEM, they need to be competent. But they need to be feminine in order to be likable"

  • Women in fields dominated by men are expected to be competitive, but being a female is a barrier for this trait. If women try to act more masculine to try to reach this competent expectation, they won't be likable. We need to acknowledge that women can be as competent as men and try to change this idea of being femenine to be the perfect woman.

    • "Women who work in STEM are doing a huge effort to get where they are. Men are by nature talented and intelligent, so it's easier for them to succeed in these areas."

  • This is partially true when stating that women are doing a huge effort to be recognized in the STEM area. However, this effort has no relation to their capacities and talent. This effort is done, because women aren't given credit for the achievements and skills they have. Women need to be acknowledge by their talent and capacity to be innovative and intelligent as well.Intelligence or being good in STEM field has nothing to do with gender.

    • Proving themselves over and over again and high-expectations

  • Women and minorities have to prove themselves, because they live in the fear of being cut off. As groups who represent a minority in the tech industry, the standards are very high and they are often put into doubt when it comes to their performance.

    • The lack of opportunities for minorities

  • Minorities tend to have scarce oportunities when it comes to developing their proficency in STEM areas. Even though there aren't visible organizations or companies who exclude minorities, when we look percentages of hired females or other minorities the numbers are clear. Less than 50% of the people employed at big tech companies are women.

    • Feeling excluded or having impostor syndrome

  • In a men dominated field, it's not weird to find a lot of women or other minorities feeling excluded were chosen for this type of jobs. A lot of women have also faced sexist comments and in some of the worst cases they have been victims of sexual misconduct/assault/abuse at work. Although this type of situations happen in any field, searching for help or a co-worker to believe in them is really hard if the amount of women working in a company is very low.
